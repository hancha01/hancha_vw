The Beauty of
Traditional Teaware

The way to enjoy tea with a long history more deliciously is to use utensils suitable for the purpose. Drinking tea has traditionally signified an act of spiritual cultivation and has long been established as a form of etiquette.

Therefore, using tea-related utensils in a manner appropriate to the purpose and method can also be considered a form of etiquette.

  • The Grace of
    Tranditional Teapots

    1. Dagwan: The primary kettle for steeping tea.

    2. Sookwoo: A vessel for pouring off tea or cooling it down to prevent it from becoming too strong.

    3. Toesuban: Used for discarding water, tea leaves, and other waste.

    4. Chaho: A jar for storing tea leaves.

    5. Chachik: A spoon used for scooping tea leaves.

    6. Chajan: A cup for drinking steeped tea.

    7. Daban: A tray or platform to place teapots.

    8. Chapo: A cloth or mat for placing on the tea table or tray.
